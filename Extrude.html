Literacy in Africa
In the context of absolute population density

This map shows the literacy rates (color) of five African countries (Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, Rwanda, South Africa) over time and in the context of either absolute population counts or population density (extrusion height). The aim of this map is to visualise the spatial differences and temporal development of the literacy rates and their possible relation to population numbers. It also illustrates the difference between absolute population counts and population density and how important it is to know which measure one is talking about.

In the current view, the volume of the columns corresponds to the absolute population count (or estimates, if no census data is available).

Clicking on one of the columns, shows further information about that specific area; such as e.g., name or the exact literacy/population values.

To access the map showing absolute population numbers, click here or use the button in the header.
